What you have
EV_ABS ABS_MT_PRESSURE (003a) → controller’s digitized “electrical pressure” (0–50+ up to 0–255).
EV_ABS ABS_MT_TOUCH_MAJOR (0030) → size of the contact ellipse, in controller units (0–50+).
EV_ABS ABS_MT_POSITION_X/Y → pixel coordinates.
EV_KEY BTN_TOUCH DOWN/UP → touch lifecycle.
EV_ABS ABS_MT_TRACKING_ID → new finger ID vs ffffffff (release).
EV_SYN SYN_REPORT → frame boundary (all values reported together).

1- Core biophysical signal carriers (highest priority)
These are non-negotiable. If iron deficiency shows up anywhere, it will show up here first.
Mutual capacitance (spatial + vascular coupling)
run_delta_read_all
get_delta
run_rawcap_read_all
get_rawcap
run_reference_read_all
get_reference

Why these matter
Delta captures dynamic changes in tissue coupling
Rawcap captures absolute dielectric properties
Reference reveals baseline shifts and drift
Iron deficiency plausibly affects:
peripheral perfusion
blood volume fraction
effective dielectric constant of fingertip tissue
These commands are your primary signal space.

2- Self-capacitance (blood + hydration sensitivity)
Often more sensitive to physiological state than mutual cap.
run_self_rawcap_read_all
run_self_delta_read_all
run_self_reference_read_all
Why
Self-cap electrodes are directly influenced by:
skin hydration
blood pooling
tissue conductivity
Iron deficiency → anemia → altered oxygen delivery → altered microcirculation
Self-cap sees this earlier than mutual cap.


3- Signal stability & micro-variability (very important, underused)
These do not measure level, but consistency. This is where subtle conditions often emerge.
run_rawdata_stdev_read
run_rawdata_p2p_read_all
run_rawcap_gap_read_all
run_rawcap_high_freq_read_all
Why
Iron deficiency is associated (indirectly) with:
reduced vascular responsiveness
altered autonomic regulation
increased micro-instability under pressure
These commands let you study:
temporal jitter
spatial unevenness
high-frequency noise vs physiological modulation
Many “negative” results become positive here.

4- Force / pressure proxies (secondary but relevant)
Iron deficiency does not change force, but it changes how tissue responds to force.
force_touch_active
run_force_calibration
get_force_calibration
run_fs_cal_pre_press
run_fs_cal_get_data
run_fs_cal_post_press
Why
Capacitive force inference depends on:
tissue compressibility
blood displacement under pressure
recovery dynamics
Iron deficiency may alter post-press recovery, not peak force.


5- Wet / hydration discrimination (critical confound control)
These do not detect iron deficiency directly, but they are essential to separate hydration from hematology.
set_wet_mode_enable
get_wet_mode
set_noise_mode_enable
Why
Hydration changes capacitance strongly.
Iron deficiency effects are weaker and easily masked.
You need these to:
model hydration explicitly
remove false positives
build hydration-invariant features

6- Temporal behavior summaries (weak labels, but useful)
These are firmware interpretations, not raw physics—but that’s exactly why they’re interesting.
holding_time
multi_count
all_touch_count
force_recal_count
Why
Iron deficiency may subtly influence:
fatigue
steadiness
micro-hesitation
recalibration frequency (due to unstable coupling)
These are secondary features, not primary evidence.


7- Commands you should explicitly exclude (to stay honest)
These are not physiologically meaningful for iron deficiency and should not be framed as such:
UI / mode toggles (aod_enable, spay_enable, dex_enable)
Geometry only (get_x_num, get_y_num)
Manufacturing / fault tests (run_trx_short_test, ito_check)
Firmware identity (fw_version, checksum)
